I Journal I — Paris

The Embarkation

AB Signature Paris was born of an airport more than an address. Paris, for A. and B., is not a headquarters: it is the gate one leaves from — the night beginning beyond a cabin window, the promise of an island at dawn. Two devotees of niche perfumery, two temperaments, one direction. They chose to give only their initials: the rest belongs to the perfumes.

The Centaur
Two temperaments, one mount. The bull — quiet strength, patience. The archer — momentum, aim, horizon.

II Journal II — Two or three departures a year

The Journeys

The rhythm of the House is set like a breath: two or three departures a year, never more. A journey must be earned — awaited, prepared, deserved. In that waiting, desire is born; on the return, the notebooks fill.

April 2023, French Polynesia: three weeks beneath the starry vault, and the sacred night of Moorea entering the notebook, never to leave it. November 2023, the Seychelles: granite and light, the high noon of La Digue — white, calm, exact. April 2024, Aruba: two weeks at the end of the trade winds, the sunset setting the sea ablaze.

Then the Mediterranean and the Atlantic: Sardinia in the summer of 2024, the Algarve in autumn, Fuerteventura in the depth of winter — their trade-wind coup de cœur —, Crete in spring 2025, Santorini in summer. Eight islands in two years. Three have already given a perfume; the others await their hour, somewhere in the pages.

From islands one brings back what cannot be packed: hours, light, scents.

III Journal III — The winter of the brief

The Brief

Two years of notebooks grow heavy. One winter, they become a document: six creations imagined, three versions asked of each — eighteen blotters asked of the nose. The brief concedes nothing: extrait de parfum, eight to twelve hours of longevity demanded on skin, strict compliance. Then the brief left for the perfumer.

IV Journal IV — Grasse

The Hand

Guillaume Cresp, in Grasse, answered with eighteen proposals. The rule of the House allows no appeal: a perfume must win you at the very first encounter, or disappear. There was no second trial. Three references survived — not one more.

Time did the rest. Four to six weeks of maceration, without haste: the extrait binds, deepens, finds its base. The creations were worn for months by those closest to the founders — on skin, at every hour — before being signed. The bottles were designed within the House; the bottling is done by their own hands. Nothing was left to chance.

If a juice does not win you over and stirs no emotion, it must be thrown away.

The rule of the House
